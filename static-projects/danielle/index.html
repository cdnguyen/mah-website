Danielle Buonaiuto Soprano

About

Danielle Buonaiuto appears throughout the United States and Canada, singing repertoire ranging from art songs to opera roles to contemporary chamber works. From Bach Passions and Mozart masses to Berio’s Folk Songs and Golijov’s Ayre, her concert repertoire spans 3 centuries, and she frequently premieres music written especially for her.

In 2011, she made her debut with the Baltimore Symphony Orchestra as Knabe 1 in Die Zauberflöte. During the 2012-2013 season, she was the featured soloist with the Lunar Ensemble in their US tour of Pierrot Lunaire and 16 new chamber works. In the spring of 2014, she returns to her native Canada for a two-month tour as Musetta in La Bohème with Jeunesses Musicales Canada. She made an appearance at the international competition Das Lied in February, and will be at the Caramoor Festival in the summer of 2013 as a Bel Canto Young Artist.

Outside of opera, Danielle revels in chamber music and art song, and frequently premieres the work of emerging composers. This season's commission project is a song cycle from Colin Read on poetry by Lisa Rosinsky, which premieres in May 2013.

When she is not travelling, writing and singing, Danielle is probably trying a new recipe, riding her bike, or buried in a great book. (Recipes, riding buddies and reading suggestions are welcome!)
